Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA)

CIP: 51.3902 | 90 Hours

This program provides the basic understanding and skills required for nursing assistants working in nursing homes, hospitals, medical clinics, and home care agencies.

Program Structure

  • 50 hours of lecture and lab work.
  • 40 hours of clinical experience in a skilled nursing facility.

Certification

Upon completion, students are eligible to take a state-administered skills exam at our school and a written exam at a local PSI Testing Center.

Career Outlook

Nursing Assisting is a foundational healthcare occupation with steady demand due to an aging population. With formal training and certification, graduates can expect to earn a median annual wage of approximately $39,400 (roughly $19 per hour). While employment growth is projected at a stable 2%, the volume of openings remains high, offering a reliable entry point into healthcare with clear opportunities for advancement into Licensed Practical Nursing (LPN) or Registered Nursing (RN) roles.

Please Note

Program availability is subject to a minimum enrollment of 20 students.

Certified Medical Assistant (CMA)

CIP: 51.0801 | 480 Hours (8-12 Months)

This comprehensive program prepares students for both clinical and administrative roles in a medical setting. The course includes a valuable externship in a physician’s office, hospital, or other healthcare facility.

Key Skills Covered

  • Anatomy, Physiology, and Medical Terminology
  • Record Keeping, Insurance Processing, and Office Practices
  • Laboratory Techniques, Phlebotomy, and EKG
  • Patient Relations, Medical Law, and Ethics
  • Taking Medical Histories, Recording Vital Signs, and First Aid

Certification

Graduates can choose to take their certification exam online through the National Healthcare Association (NHA) or in-person via MedCa.

Career Outlook

Medical Assisting is one of the fastest-growing healthcare occupations, with employment projected to grow 12% over the next decade—much faster than the national average. With formal training and certification, graduates can expect to earn a median annual wage of approximately $44,200 (roughly $21 per hour), with experienced earners exceeding $56,000. The role offers dynamic opportunities for advancement into healthcare administration, specialized clinical nursing, or office management roles.

Phlebotomy Technician

CIP: 51.1009 | 120 Hours

This course covers the essential practices and procedures for phlebotomists, focusing on the safe and effective collection of blood samples.

Key Skills Covered

  • Universal Precautions and Infection Control
  • Capillary Sticks, Skin Puncture, and Venipuncture
  • Butterfly Techniques and Multiple Tube Drawings
  • Specimen Handling, Transportation, and Processing
  • Medical Law, Ethics, and Patient Confidentiality

Certification

Graduates can choose to take their certification exam online through the National Healthcare Association (NHA) or in-person via MedCa.

Career Outlook

Phlebotomy is a rapidly expanding field, with employment projected to grow 6% over the next decade—faster than the average for all occupations. With formal training and certification, graduates can expect to earn a median annual wage of approximately $43,600 (roughly $21 per hour). As an essential part of diagnostic teams, phlebotomists have excellent opportunities for advancement into roles such as Medical Laboratory Technician, Histotechnician, or Registered Nurse.

EKG Technician

CIP: 51.0902 | 120 Hours (6 Weeks)

This 6-week course focuses on the technical skills required to perform electrocardiograms (EKGs), including machine operation and interpretation. The program includes a 100-hour externship.

Key Skills Covered

  • Cardiovascular Anatomy and Physiology
  • EKG Machine Operation and Graph Interpretation
  • 12-Lead EKG Placement and Artifact Recognition
  • Pharmacology of Cardiac Diseases
  • Patient Education and Safety

Certification

Graduates can choose to take their certification exam online through the National Healthcare Association (NHA) or in-person via MedCa.

Career Outlook

EKG Technology is a specialized technical field with competitive earning potential for entry-level graduates. With formal training and certification, graduates typically earn between $42,000 and $54,000 annually (roughly $20 - $26 per hour), depending on the facility. The role serves as a vital stepping stone in cardiac care, offering clear pathways for advancement into higher-level positions such as Cardiovascular Technologist, Telemetry Technician, or Cardiac Monitor Technician.
